Minotaur

The Minotaur is a creature from Greek mythology with the body of a man and the head of a bull. He was born from the union of Queen PasiphaÃ« and a divine bull sent by Poseidon.

King Minos of Crete, ashamed of the creature, ordered the architect Daedalus to build a vast labyrinth beneath his palace to imprison the Minotaur. Every year, young Athenians were sent as tribute to be sacrificed to the beast.

The hero Theseus volunteered to end this suffering. With the help of Ariadne, who gave him a ball of thread, he entered the labyrinth, slew the Minotaur, and found his way out using the thread.

The Labyrinth
